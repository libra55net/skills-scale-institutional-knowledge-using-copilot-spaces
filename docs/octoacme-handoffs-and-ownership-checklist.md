# OctoAcme — Handoffs & Ownership Checklist

## Purpose
Reduce ambiguity when work moves between planning, implementation, validation, and release.

## When to use this checklist
- At kickoff for any cross-functional initiative
- Before moving backlog items into active delivery
- Before QA, release, or customer-facing launch handoffs
- Any time a decision is blocked because ownership is unclear

## Ownership Alignment
- [ ] Product Manager named for scope, priorities, and success metrics
- [ ] Project Manager named for schedule, coordination, and risk follow-up
- [ ] Delivery owner named for implementation work
- [ ] QA / Test owner named for validation and exit criteria
- [ ] Supporting owners named for design, platform, security, or support needs
- [ ] Decision owner named for each open trade-off or escalation

## Handoff Readiness
- [ ] Problem statement and desired outcome are documented
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are specific enough to validate
- [ ] Dependencies, approvals, and environment needs are identified
- [ ] Risks are logged with an owner and next mitigation step
- [ ] Required docs, designs, or links are attached
- [ ] The receiving team confirms what "done" means for the handoff

## Stage-Based Prompts

### Planning -> Delivery
- [ ] Scope is prioritized and the next increment is small enough to deliver
- [ ] Developers have the context, decisions, and dependencies needed to start
- [ ] Designers, QA, and platform partners know when they are expected to engage

### Delivery -> QA
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are mapped to validation steps
- [ ] Known limitations, feature flags, and test data needs are documented
- [ ] Defect triage owner and turnaround expectations are clear

### QA -> Release
- [ ] Exit criteria are met or explicitly waived by the decision owner
- [ ] Rollback, monitoring, and support readiness have named owners
- [ ] Customer or stakeholder communications are prepared if needed

### Release -> Follow-up
- [ ] Success metrics and early signals are being monitored
- [ ] Customer feedback path is defined
- [ ] Remaining follow-up items have owners and due dates
